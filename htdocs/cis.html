Cisgender

Cisgender is a gender identity where a person's identity matches the one a doctor assigned them at birth. For example, if the sex assigned to a person is male, and the person identifies as male, he is a cisgender man.

Of course, a cisgender person can still be a part of the LGBTQIA+ community. Being cisgender is a gender identity, not a sexual or romantic attraction. This means that a cisgender individual does not have to be heterosexual.

Being called cis is not derogatory in any way. It's just a way of describing someone's gender identity. It's like describing a trans person as trans. It's not wrong in any way. "I don't want to be called cis, I want to be called a man/woman." This statement implies that transmen and transwomen aren't normal. Refusing to be called a cis roots from internalized transphobia.
