Non-Binary

Nonbinary is a term used by people who aren't exclusively male or female. They can be between or beyond the binary genders. Nonbinary individuals may be genderfluid, agender, genderqueer, or something else entirely.

All non-binary people fall under the transgender umbrella. However, not all transgender people are non-binary since most transgender people identify within the gender binary. Take note that some non-binary individuals might not be comfortable with being identified as transgender, so please make sure to ask them first.

Non-binary people are not necessarily androgynous, so do not assume. They can be as masculine or feminine as they want; this does not erase their identity. Just as you would for a transman or transwoman, use the name they want you to use; do not ask for their dead name if they have a different name.

People might think that all non-binary people use "they/them" pronouns. This may be true for most non-binary people, but some also use "he" or "she" pronouns, maybe even other pronouns. It is safest if one asks someone's preferred pronouns.
