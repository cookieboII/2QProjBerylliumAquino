Transgender

Transgender is an umbrella term for people who do not conform to their assigned sex at birth. Transgender persons express their gender identities differently. Some show their gender identities through the way they dress, act, and behave. Some go through what is called Gender Reassignment Surgery. Some are androgynous, while some do not even alter their appearance. However, it is still important to ask someone's pronouns as this is a form of respect and support to one's identity.

Being transgender does not indicate any specific sexual orientation. A transgender person can still be straight, gay, lesbian, bisexual, pansexual, etc.

Some people's gender identities cannot be defined by the gender binary. They are called enbys or non-binaries. They have an incredibly diverse community. They may be comfortable with binary pronouns, but they can also use the gender-neutral term, they/them.
